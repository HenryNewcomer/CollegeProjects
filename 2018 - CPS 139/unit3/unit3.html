My 3 Favorite Topics

Computer Programming

When I was thirteen, a friend rented an HTML book from the library and showed me it one day. After borrowing it from him, I was hooked. I started a couple different fan-sites for video games, one of which lasted about nine years, on-and-off. This also sparked me into looking at other computer languages to dabble in - several of which have helped me find freelance work (such as my current job). To date, I've used:

  • Client Side languages
    • Website-related
      • HTML4/XHTML/HTML5
      • CSS/CSS3
      • JavaScript
      • ActionScript
    • Desktop environment
      • Java
      • C++
  • Server Side languages
    • PHP
    • SQL

Digital Art

Websites (especially certain "fan-sites") used to have a lot more graphical identity than they do today. The standard Web 2.0 appearance of everything gives most sites a similar look and feel, nowadays. However, I still find enjoyment in trying to provide a special identity in the graphical look of websites. I also recently got a digital drawing tablet for my computer so I've been having fun messing around with digital painting.

I prefer digital art compared to traditional art for the following reasons:

  1. No mess
  2. Accidents happen; digital canvases have arrays of undos
  3. A quicker and easier access to blending & gradient options
  4. Artwork can be saved with layers for easier editing
  5. Unlimited backups can be saved; no physical, excessive buildup

Video Games

Although I didn't get my first game system until I was in sixth grade (a GameBoy Color), I loved going over to friends' houses to play video games with them. Once I saw a friend playing Zelda: The Ocarina of Time on their Nintendo 64, I was blown away at how video games could be experienced. There's a long list of history I could write past that point, but as I've grown up, I've played games less and less (yet still try new ones out from time-to-time). For quite a while now, I've mostly played Destiny (1 & 2) and have made some really close friends. It's amazing how technology has evolved and created new situations that couldn't have been possible in the past.
