One Paragraph About Myself

Hello, I'm Henry. Currently, I'm 29 and live in Fond du Lac, Wisconsin. The first dog that I've ever owned just recently turned a year old, and I wouldn't trade him for anything. After years of thinking that college wouldn't be right for me, I finally decided to give it a go - and now, here I am.

My 3 Favorite Topics

After ~17 years of experience, it's safe to say that I love computer programming languages. I enjoy creating digital art, both as a form of "drawing/painting" and for website design. I've considered myself to be an avid gamer, but I'm really only addicted to the "Destiny" franchise, now.

More About My Topics

Programming. When I was thirteen, a friend rented an HTML book from the library and showed me it one day. After borrowing it from him, I was hooked. I started a couple different fan-sites for video games, one of which lasted about nine years, on-and-off. This also sparked me into looking at other computer languages to dabble in - several of which have helped me find freelance work (such as my current job). To date, I've used HTML4/XHTML/HTML5, CSS/CSS3, PHP, JavaScript, Java, ActionScript, SQL, and lately, C++.

Digital Art. Websites (especially certain "fan-sites") used to have a lot more graphical identity than they do today. The standard Web 2.0 appearance of everything gives most sites a similar look and feel, nowadays. However, I still find enjoyment in trying to provide a special identity in the graphical look of websites. I also recently got a digital drawing tablet for my computer so I've been having fun messing around with digital painting.

Video Games. Although I didn't get my first game system until I was in sixth grade (a GameBoy Color), I loved going over to friends' houses to play video games with them. Once I saw a friend playing Zelda: The Ocarina of Time on their Nintendo 64, I was blown away at how video games could be experienced. There's a long list of history I could write past that point, but as I've grown up, I've played games less and less (yet still try new ones out from time-to-time). For quite a while now, I've mostly played Destiny (1 & 2) and have made some really close friends. It's amazing how technology has evolved and created new situations that couldn't have been possible in the past.
